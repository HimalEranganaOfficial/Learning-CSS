Stephen William Hawking

The Universe Unraveler

Theoretical physicist professor Stephen Hawking is pictured during his lecture on the creation of the Universe at the European Organization for Nuclear Research (CERN) in Meyrin near Geneva September 9, 2009. Valentin Flauraud/Reuters

Here's a time line of Stephen William Hawking's life:

  • 1942: Born in Oxford, England, on January 8th.
  • 1952: Begins his secondary education at St Albans School.
  • 1959: Receives a scholarship to study at University College, Oxford, starting in October.
  • 1962: Graduates from Oxford with a degree in Physics and starts his postgraduate studies in Cosmology at Cambridge.
  • 1963: Diagnosed with Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS), also known as Lou Gehrig's disease. Doctors initially predict he has only two years to live.
  • 1965: Marries Jane Wilde.
  • 1966: Earns his Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics and Theoretical Physics from Cambridge.
  • 1970: Begins to develop his theories about black holes and singularities.
  • 1974: Hawking's groundbreaking paper on black hole radiation (Hawking radiation) is published, revolutionizing the understanding of black holes.
  • 1979: Becomes Lucasian Professor of Mathematics at Cambridge University, a prestigious position once held by Isaac Newton.
  • 1985: Loses his ability to speak after a tracheotomy. He begins to use a speech synthesizer to communicate.
  • 1988: Publishes "A Brief History of Time," which becomes a global bestseller and makes cosmology accessible to the masses.
  • 1989: Made a Companion of Honour.
  • 1995: Divorces Jane Wilde and marries Elaine Mason.
  • 2004: Hawking concedes a long-standing bet, admitting that black holes do destroy information, contrary to his earlier theory.
  • 2007: Experiences zero-gravity on a specially modified Boeing 727, fulfilling a long-held dream.
  • 2009: Steps down as Lucasian Professor of Mathematics at Cambridge, becoming a Research Professor.
  • 2014: His biopic "The Theory of Everything," starring Eddie Redmayne, is released to critical acclaim.
  • 2018: Stephen Hawking passes away on March 14th at the age of 76, leaving behind a monumental legacy in science and inspiration.

Sir Roger Penrose,

renowned mathematical physicist,
Nobel Laureate in Physics (2020),

To truly appreciate the incredible journey of this extraordinary scientist, consider watching "The Theory of Everything", a moving biographical movie that portrays Stephen Hawking's life, his love story with Jane Wilde, and his battles with ALS. It offers a powerful glimpse into his world and the strength of his spirit.
